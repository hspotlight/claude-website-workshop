The Problem

You read like you did in third grade.

Nobody ever taught you how to read — only what to read. So you still subvocalize every word, your eyes wander back to lines you already finished, and a 300-page book takes a month you don't have.

Meanwhile your reading list grows faster than you can shrink it. Reports pile up. Books gather dust. The knowledge you need sits unread — and every year the gap gets wider.

The frustrating part? Your brain can process text far faster than your current habits allow. The bottleneck isn't intelligence. It's technique.

The Solution

A trainable skill, broken into three moves.

Reading Fast rebuilds your reading mechanics from the ground up. Each technique comes with timed drills and a daily practice schedule — 15 minutes a day for 21 days.

Silence the inner voice

Subvocalization caps you at speaking speed. Learn displacement drills that quiet the voice in your head so your eyes — not your mouth — set the pace.

Widen your eye span

Stop reading word-by-word. Train your eyes to take in chunks of 3–5 words per fixation using pacing drills and peripheral-vision exercises.

Kill regression

Break the habit of re-reading with pointer techniques and comprehension checkpoints — so you move forward once, and retain what you read.
